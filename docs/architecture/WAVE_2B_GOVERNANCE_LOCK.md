Purpose

This document locks the Governance Foundation of TiMELiNES.

Governance defines how authority is reviewed, approved, disputed, revised, published, preserved, and audited.

Governance does not create historical knowledge.

Governance controls the institutional processes through which historical knowledge becomes authoritative.

This document establishes the permanent governance doctrine of the platform.

⸻

Governance Mission

The purpose of Governance is:

Protect Authority
Protect Published Memory
Protect Historical Integrity
Protect Auditability
Protect Preservation

Governance exists to ensure that authority changes are deliberate, reviewable, reconstructable, and preservable.

Governance is not a presentation layer.

Governance is not a search layer.

Governance is not a content-generation layer.

Governance is an institutional layer.

⸻

Candidates

Platform

Governance Rule

The following rule is permanently locked.

No authority mutation may occur without:

Evidence
    ↓
Approval (when required)
    ↓
Governance Decision
    ↓
Registry or Library Execution
    ↓
Audit Record

Any workflow that bypasses this sequence violates platform doctrine.

Publication Doctrine

Publication is governed by the following rule:

Factory proposes
Governance certifies
Historical Library accepts
Platform presents

This sequence is mandatory.

No institution may bypass it.

⸻

Publication Readiness Doctrine

Authority is publishable only when:

* Validation passed
* Provenance exists
* Required approvals exist
* No blocking disputes exist
* Audit reconstruction is possible
* Governance readiness certification exists

Publication readiness is not determined by Factory.

Publication readiness is not determined by Platform.

Publication readiness is certified through Governance.

⸻

Dispute Doctrine

Disputes are permanent governance artifacts.

Disputes:

* Are never deleted
* Preserve evidence
* Preserve rationale
* Preserve outcomes

Disputes do not directly change authority.

Disputes resolve through Governance Decisions.

⸻

Audit Doctrine

Every authority-changing action must answer:

Who changed it?
Why was it changed?
What evidence supported it?
Who approved it?
What state existed before?
What state exists now?
What package, dispute, or feedback records were involved?

If these questions cannot be answered, governance has failed.

⸻

Preservation Doctrine

Governance inherits the platform no-delete doctrine.

The following must remain reconstructable:

* Decisions
* Approvals
* Packages
* Disputes
* Audit Records
* Authority State Changes

Governance history is permanent.
