FOUNDER_OPERATING_SYSTEM.md

TiMELiNES Founder Operating System (FOS)

Version 1.0

Status: LOCK

Authority Level: Tier 1

⸻

Purpose

This document defines the operational experience of the Founder.

It is the constitutional authority governing how the Founder interacts with TiMELiNES.

It does not define institutional implementation.

It does not define engineering.

It defines the operational model through which the Founder runs a continuously operating historical publishing institution.

All Founder-facing interfaces shall conform to this document.

⸻

Mission

The Founder should operate TiMELiNES as a publishing institution.

The Founder should never need to understand the underlying implementation.

Institutional complexity shall remain internal.

Operational simplicity shall remain external.

⸻

Core Philosophy

The Founder supplies ideas.

The institution produces historical knowledge.

The platform executes the process.

⸻

Foundational Principle

The Founder operates the institution.

The Founder does not operate software.

The Founder does not operate APIs.

The Founder does not operate institutional implementation.

⸻

Operational Object

The single operational object is:

Topic

Everything begins as a Topic.

Every Topic follows one institutional lifecycle.

No parallel operational models shall exist.

⸻

Topic Sources

A Topic may originate from exactly one source.

Founder

Topics manually entered by the Founder.

Examples:

* Telephone
* Steam Engine
* Apollo Program

⸻

Factory

Topics suggested automatically by the Factory.

Suggestions are based on:

* historical gaps
* source availability
* visitor demand
* timeline relationships
* editorial opportunities
* institutional intelligence

Factory Suggestions do not automatically enter production.

They require acceptance.

⸻

Visitor

Topics originating from visitor requests.

Visitor requests become production Topics only after approval.

⸻

Unified Production Queue

Every accepted Topic enters one unified production queue.

There shall never be multiple operational queues for different Topic sources.

The queue is the operational heart of TiMELiNES.

⸻

Continuous Institution Law

The Factory is a continuously operating institution.

While running it continuously:

* discovers Topics
* researches
* extracts
* validates
* prepares publication
* publishes
* improves institutional knowledge

The Factory shall never stop because one Topic requires human review.

Topics waiting for review are isolated.

The remaining queue continues processing.

⸻

Human Attention Law

Human attention is exceptional.

The Founder participates only when human judgement is required.

Examples include:

* Editorial Review
* Governance Review
* Evidence Conflict
* Publication Verification Failure
* Operational Exception

Everything else shall execute autonomously.

⸻

Founder Inbox Law

All human-required work belongs inside one unified Founder Inbox.

The Inbox shall contain only actionable work.

Examples:

* Approve
* Return for Revision
* Reject
* Retry
* Dismiss

The Founder should never perform routine operational work outside the Inbox.

⸻

Operations Home

The Founder lands on Home.

Home is the operational center of TiMELiNES.

Home answers one question:

What requires my attention today?

Home shall provide immediate visibility into:

* institution status
* active Topics
* queue
* Founder Inbox
* Factory Suggestions
* recent publications
* activity feed
* operational health

Queue

⸻

Queue

Purpose:

Observe institutional production.

The Queue represents every Topic currently under production.

Queue sections include:

* Running
* Queued
* Waiting Review
* Published Today
* Failed
* Cancelled

Each Topic displays:

* title
* origin
* status
* progress
* estimated completion
* priority

Queue language shall remain operational.

Institutional implementation shall remain hidden.

⸻

Library

Purpose:

Observe published knowledge.

The Library contains:

* Timelines
* Historical Objects
* Milestones
* Relationships
* Sources
* Collections
* Coverage

The Library represents published institutional knowledge.

It is editorial rather than operational.

⸻

Analytics

Purpose:

Understand visitors.

Analytics includes:

* visitor behavior
* searches
* reading activity
* engagement
* popular Topics
* failed searches
* publication performance
* growth

Analytics measures the public platform rather than institutional implementation.

⸻

Settings

Settings configure institutional behavior.

Examples include:

* Factory Mode
* Automation
* Notifications
* Providers
* Appearance

Settings never expose operational implementation.

⸻

Diagnostics

Diagnostics exists exclusively for investigation.

Diagnostics contains:

* Factory internals
* Governance internals
* Historical Library internals
* Published Memory
* Projection
* Workers
* Scheduler
* Replay
* Provider State
* APIs
* Logs
* JSON
* Package identifiers
* Internal metrics

Normal Founder operation should rarely require Diagnostics.

⸻

Factory Modes

The Founder may configure one Factory Mode.

Conservative

The Factory processes Topics automatically.

Topics requiring review enter the Founder Inbox.

Publication continues for all remaining Topics.

⸻

Autonomous

The Factory publishes automatically whenever governance policies permit.

Only exceptional situations enter the Founder Inbox.

⸻

Maintenance

The Factory finishes currently executing Topics.

No new Topics begin processing until resumed.

⸻

Factory Suggestions

Factory Suggestions represent institutional intelligence.

Each suggestion shall display:

* Topic
* Reason
* Confidence
* Estimated authority quality

Available actions:

* Queue
* Dismiss
* Ignore

Suggestions remain outside production until accepted.

⸻

Visitor Requests

Visitor requests represent editorial demand.

Approved requests become Topics.

Every Topic permanently retains its origin:

Founder

Factory

Visitor

Origin remains visible throughout production.

⸻

Activity Feed

The institution continuously reports significant events.

Examples:

* Topic entered Research
* Extraction completed
* Governance Review required
* Publication completed
* Factory Suggestion generated
* Operational alert created

The Activity Feed communicates institutional activity without exposing implementation.

⸻

Separation of Concerns

Institutional architecture remains unchanged.

Operational experience remains independent.

The Founder Operating System presents a simplified operational read model over certified institutional architecture.

Implementation details shall never dictate Founder experience.

⸻

Screen Law

Every screen answers exactly one question.

Home

What requires my attention today?

Queue

What is the institution producing?

Library

What has been published?

Analytics

How are visitors using TiMELiNES?

Settings

How should the institution behave?

Diagnostics

Why did something fail?

If a screen attempts to answer multiple unrelated questions, it shall be redesigned.
